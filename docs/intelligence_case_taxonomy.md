# Intelligence Case Taxonomy

This taxonomy defines how the Strategic Intelligence Agent organizes historical outcome cases for strategic intelligence review. The case database is educational and deterministic. It supports comparison, pattern recognition, and decision-support discussion; it does not provide forecasts, legal advice, investment advice, or claims of real-world accuracy.

## Case Families

| Family | What It Captures | Typical Mechanisms |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Export Controls | Restrictions on technology, equipment, software, customers, or end users | Technology Containment; Market Access Restriction; Compliance Burden; Counterparty Risk |
| Sanctions | Restrictions on entities, sectors, payments, assets, trade, or counterparties | Counterparty Risk; Market Access Restriction; Compliance Burden; Financial Channel Restriction |
| Industrial Policy | Subsidies, tax credits, public capacity programs, domestic-content rules | Industrial Subsidy; Strategic Dependency; Capital Allocation; Compliance Burden |
| Supply Chain Disruption | Logistics shocks, chokepoints, shortages, supplier failures, route uncertainty | Supply Chain Reconfiguration; Strategic Dependency; Information Uncertainty; Operational Resilience |
| Technology Competition | Strategic competition around chips, AI, cloud, telecom, software, or data | Technology Containment; Strategic Dependency; Platform Control; Market Access Restriction |
| Regulatory Action | Rules, enforcement, compliance obligations, platform regulation, financial regulation | Regulatory Shock; Compliance Burden; Market Access Restriction; Governance Constraint |
| Geopolitical Escalation | Security tensions, conflict exposure, maritime risk, energy route uncertainty | Security Escalation; Supply Chain Reconfiguration; Information Uncertainty; Counterparty Risk |
| Corporate Strategic Response | Firm-level responses to geopolitical, policy, or operating shocks | Supplier Diversification; Market Exit; Governance Escalation; Exposure Mapping |
| Financial / Earnings Geopolitical Exposure | Earnings or financial disclosures shaped by sanctions, regional exposure, or compliance burden | Compliance Burden; Counterparty Risk; Market Access Restriction; Management Disclosure |

## Confidence Labels

| Label | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| High | Broad outcome pattern is widely documented in public summaries, but the row remains an educational simplification. |
| Medium | Outcome pattern is plausible and useful for comparison, but details should be checked against primary sources. |
| Low | Outcome pattern is emerging, indirect, generalized, or highly dependent on event-specific details. |

## Dataset Guardrails

- Source URLs are not fabricated.
- Missing source links remain marked as `source pending`.
- Historical outcomes are simplified educational summaries.
- Confidence labels are internal evidence-coding labels, not probabilities.
- Cases support structured comparison; they do not imply repeatable future outcomes.
