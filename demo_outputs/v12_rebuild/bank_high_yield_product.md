# V12 Demo Output: Bank High-Yield Product Promotion

## Direct Answer

- **What happened:** A bank promoted a high-yield customer product, so the event is treated as Financial Product Risk.
- **Why it matters:** Product design, suitability, liquidity, disclosure, and customer communication can become strategic and regulatory issues.
- **Comparison guardrail:** Compare with banking supervision, customer-risk, product-governance, and financial disclosure cases.

## Similar Cases

- Bank Stress Liquidity Disclosure.
- Bank Capital Rule Debate.
- Sanctions Compliance Expense Disclosure when compliance operating burden is relevant.

## What Happened Then

- Financial institutions often reviewed disclosure language, liquidity assumptions, customer segmentation, and supervisory communication.

## How Organizations Responded

- Banks reviewed risk controls, compliance staffing, customer documentation, and board-level reporting.

## What Happened After

- Similar cases often shifted from product promotion to governance questions about customer understanding, liquidity, and control evidence.

## Market Expectations vs Actual Outcomes

- Expected outcome: a product-led deposit or customer acquisition story.
- Observed outcome in comparable local cases: oversight, liquidity, and disclosure questions can become more important than promotion language.
- Lesson: monitor customer risk and control evidence, not only product yield.

## What To Watch Next

- Investor: deposit behavior, liquidity commentary, regulatory cost.
- Corporate Strategy: product governance, customer segmentation, escalation paths.
- Supply Chain: not central unless vendor or platform dependency is involved.
- Policy: supervisory guidance, disclosure standards, consumer-risk review.

## Limitations

- This is not financial advice and does not assess product suitability for any customer.
