# Supply Chain Context KB

## Entry SP-001
industry: Supply Chain
scenario_type: Supply Chain Disruption
context_summary: Logistics disruptions can affect routing, lead times, inventory planning, and customer commitments.
why_it_matters: Operational teams may need to update contingency plans and supplier communication.
stakeholders: Logistics providers; manufacturers; retailers; customers; port operators
monitoring_considerations: Route changes; freight timing; port congestion; supplier delivery updates

## Entry SP-002
industry: Supply Chain
scenario_type: Military / Security Shock
context_summary: Security incidents near shipping corridors can create rerouting requirements and operational uncertainty.
why_it_matters: Route disruption can affect delivery schedules and working-capital planning.
stakeholders: Shippers; insurers; naval authorities; exporters; importers
monitoring_considerations: Maritime advisories; insurance terms; rerouting patterns; carrier notices

## Entry SP-003
industry: Supply Chain
scenario_type: Trade Policy
context_summary: Customs rules, tariffs, and import restrictions can affect sourcing decisions and documentation needs.
why_it_matters: Firms may need to reassess supplier terms, landed costs, and compliance controls.
stakeholders: Importers; exporters; customs brokers; procurement teams; regulators
monitoring_considerations: Tariff schedules; customs guidance; supplier contracts; documentation gaps

## Entry SP-004
industry: Supply Chain
scenario_type: Export Controls
context_summary: Export controls can restrict shipment of sensitive goods, software, or technology to certain end users.
why_it_matters: Companies may need to screen counterparties and assess license requirements before fulfillment.
stakeholders: Exporters; freight forwarders; compliance teams; customers; regulators
monitoring_considerations: Restricted party lists; product classifications; license applications; end-use statements
