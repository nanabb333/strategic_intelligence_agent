# Red Sea Shipping Disruption

## Decision Question

How should a supply-chain team assess response options when shipping disruption affects Red Sea routes and delivery reliability?

## Input Summary

The provided material describes shipping disruption affecting route reliability, transit times, insurance costs, and customer delivery expectations. Decision-makers need to understand whether to wait, reroute selectively, adjust inventory buffers, or prepare broader continuity options.

## Decision Context

This is an operational resilience and supply-chain decision-support case. The decision is shaped by uncertainty around disruption duration, cost impact, customer commitments, and available logistics alternatives. The case does not provide logistics advice or guarantee operational outcomes.

## Confidence and Assumptions

**Confidence level:** Moderate

**Key assumptions**

- The provided source material is sufficient for an initial decision-support brief.
- Route reliability and customer commitments are material decision criteria.
- Human review will validate shipment-level exposure, carrier options, and cost constraints.
- Historical disruption patterns are useful for comparison, not prediction.

**What would change this view**

- Disruption duration is materially shorter or longer than assumed.
- Freight rates, insurance terms, or carrier capacity change materially.
- Customer delivery commitments become more or less urgent.
- New source material shows the disruption affects different routes or products than assumed.

**Unknowns**

- Shipment-level exposure.
- Available alternate route capacity.
- Customer tolerance for delivery changes.
- Contractual penalties or insurance requirements.
