# Industrial Subsidy Strategy

## Decision Question

How should a strategy team evaluate whether new industrial subsidies change investment, partnership, or market-entry priorities?

## Input Summary

The provided material describes an industrial subsidy program that may affect eligibility, domestic-content requirements, supplier partnerships, capital allocation, and competitive positioning. Decision-makers need to assess whether to wait, investigate eligibility, or prepare a staged strategic response.

## Decision Context

This is an industrial policy and corporate strategy decision-support case. The relevant decision is not whether subsidies are good or bad, but how a team should structure review of eligibility, constraints, trade-offs, and monitoring signals before committing resources.

## Evidence Sufficiency and Assumptions

**Structural Evidence Sufficiency tier:** Reviewable

**Key assumptions**

- The provided material is sufficient to frame an initial strategy-review decision.
- Eligibility and implementation details remain uncertain.
- Historical industrial-policy patterns are useful for comparison, not outcome prediction.
- Human review will validate policy text, financial exposure, and operational feasibility.

**What would change this view**

- Final rules materially expand or narrow eligibility.
- Domestic-content requirements become more restrictive than assumed.
- Competitor or supplier responses change the strategic value of participation.
- Internal capital constraints make investigation or participation impractical.

**Unknowns**

- Final eligibility criteria.
- Compliance cost.
- Supplier and partner readiness.
- Competitive response.
- Firm-specific financial and operational exposure.
